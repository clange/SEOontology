Introduction

The Scientific Events Ontology (OR-SEO), with a new namespace seo, is engineered to represent metadata of scientific events. The real-world implementation of the presented ontology is OpenResearch.org. It is a systematic redesign of the vocabulary used as the schema of event pages on OpenResearch.org, reusing well-known vocabularies and following standard methodologies. OR-SEO has been developed from scratch using SAMOD, an iterative process that aims at building the final model through a series of small steps.

* for better readability, classes are displayed with their labels, i.e. rdfs:label.

Rule #2back to ToC

Agentc(?sioc:a) , holds roleop(?sioc:a,?sioc:e) -> participate inop(?sioc:a,?sioc:e)

Rule #3back to ToC

value in timec(?x) , at time(?x,?t) -> at timeop(?x,?t)

time interval(?t) , at timeop(?x,?t) -> at time(?x,?t)

has locationop(?e1,?l1) , has locationop(?e2,?l2) , end datedp(?e1,?ed1) , end datedp(?e2,?ed2) , start datedp(?e1,?sd1) , start datedp(?e2,?sd2) , equal(?ed1,?ed2) , equal(?l1,?l2) , equal(?sd1,?sd2) -> colocated withop(?e1,?e2)
